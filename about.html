About the project

Built because the lost-and-found box at the front office wasn't working anymore.

Findr is the front-end prototype for our proposed database application — a capstone project by Group 5, submitted as the Web-Based Lost-and-Found Management System with Database Connectivity for Educational Institutions.

Background

The problem we set out to solve

Lost-and-found in most schools today is handled manually — paper logs, physical drop-off boxes, and word of mouth. This creates three recurring problems: items end up scattered across different offices with no centralized tracking, nothing is photographed or documented so matching an item to its owner becomes guesswork, and students are rarely notified when their belongings are found, so items sit unclaimed for weeks.

Together, these gaps lead to low recovery rates, frustrated students, and extra workload for school staff who have to manage everything by hand.

Mission & vision

What we want this system to do

Mission

To give educational institutions a centralized, reliable, and easy-to-use platform for recording, searching, and recovering lost belongings — replacing manual logbooks with a single source of truth.

Vision

A campus where no lost item goes untracked — where students get notified the moment something is found, and staff spend less time on paperwork and more time helping people.

Purpose & objectives

Why this project exists

This activity serves as the front-end prototype of our final database application proposal.

01

Centralize records

Replace scattered logbooks with one MySQL-backed database covering users, items, categories, locations, claims, and reports.

02

Speed up recovery

Let students search by name, category, date, or location instead of checking every office in person.

03

Make claims accountable

Require proof descriptions and staff verification before any item is marked claimed, with a full audit trail.
